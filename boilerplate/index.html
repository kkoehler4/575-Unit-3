Who's Ready for Kindergarten?

Vermont's public education system is currently ranked 3rd in the country by Education Weekly. But not all kids are equally prepared to enter the education system.

How is Readiness Measured?

The R4K!S is not a direct assessment of students, but the observations from teachers over the first few weeks of school. The teacher assesses each student based on five dimensions of readiness: Social and emotional development, communication, physical health, cognitive development, knowledge, and approaches to learning (e.g., enthusiasm for learning, persistence, curiosity). Vermont’s concept also reflects the belief that “school readiness” is interactional: students need to be ready for schools, and schools need to be ready to accommodate the diverse needs of each and every child.

Teachers assess the students based on these dimensions using a four-level scale to calculate overall readiness. Students whose “Total Scores” fall into categories three and four (“Ready and Practicing” and “Ready and Performing Independently”) are identified as ready.

What does this mean for Vermont students?

Overall, 86.24% of Vermont students were deemed ready for Kindergarten in 2021. 8% more girls were ready for Kindergarten than boys, and 12% more kids not elegible for free and reduced lunch were ready than kids not elegible for free and reduced lunch.
